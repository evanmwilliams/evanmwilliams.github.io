## Classes

Take coursework that challenges you! There's no point going to a place like Cornell just to take courses you don't find very interesting or challenging. Some of my favorite courses here have been: 
* CS 4120: Intro to Compilers 
* ECE 4750: Computer Architecture
* CS 4820: Intro to Algorithms 
* CS 4780: Intro to Machine Learning 


## Clubs/Project Teams

Don't stress out too much if you don't get into your top choice project team (or any project team for that matter). I think it's important to remember that clubs are run by students, who are just as fallable and subject to error as you, the applicant, are. Project teams are great opportunities but they won't determine job outcomes or anything like that. 

To that end do what you enjoy! Find good friends and do cool things with them! There are also many avenues for doing cool things at Cornell that aren't project teams. Research, independent studies, other student organizations, etc. are all great ways to get some experience.
